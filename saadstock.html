🎓 Stock Mentor
❌ Why Designs Get Rejected & How to Fix Them
1. Technical Quality Issues
Adobe Stock requires strict technical standards. Ensure your files meet all requirements:
  • Minimum 4 megapixels resolution for photos (JPG)
  • Always embed sRGB color profile — CMYK will be rejected
  • No compression artifacts — save JPEGs at maximum quality (12 in Photoshop)
  • Vectors must have no open paths, no raster effects embedded
  • No watermarks, signatures, or logos anywhere in the file
2. Keyword & Metadata Problems
Keyword mismatch is one of the top rejection reasons. Adobe's AI reviews keywords against image content:
  • Use 10–50 relevant keywords — never keyword stuff
  • Never use brand names, celebrity names, or trademarks
  • Every keyword must genuinely describe something visible in the image
  • Include: subject + style + color + mood + use case + industry
3. Intellectual Property Issues
These will always be rejected without proper documentation:
  • Recognizable people without a signed model release
  • Private property (buildings, homes) without a property release
  • Logos, trademarks, branded products visible in the image
  • Artwork or designs based on copyrighted characters
4. Over-Saturated Content
Even technically perfect designs may get accepted but never downloaded if the niche is overcrowded. Generic sunsets, cliché handshakes, overused coffee cup shots — Adobe Stock has millions of these. Use the Market Gap Finder tool to find underserved niches before creating.
🏷️ Advanced Keywording Strategy
The 3-Layer Keyword Pyramid
Build your keywords in three layers for maximum search visibility:
  • Layer 1 — Core (1–3 words): The exact subject. "businesswoman", "coffee cup", "geometric pattern"
  • Layer 2 — Context (5–10 words): Style, color, mood, setting. "minimal", "flat design", "blue background", "isolated"
  • Layer 3 — Use Case (10–20 words): Where buyers use it. "website banner", "social media", "marketing material", "presentation"
High-Converting Keyword Categories
Always include keywords from these categories when relevant:
  • Season/Holiday: Christmas, summer, Eid, Diwali, Halloween, Ramadan
  • Style: minimalist, retro, watercolor, 3D render, flat icon, hand-drawn
  • Format: banner, background, icon set, infographic, template, pattern
  • Industry: healthcare, technology, education, finance, food, nature
  • Emotion: happy, success, teamwork, growth, calm, professional
Keywords to Never Use
These cause rejection or ranking penalties:
  • Brand names (Apple, Nike, Google)
  • Celebrity names or public figures
  • Completely unrelated words added for traffic
  • Misspellings (except common alternative spellings)
  • Overly generic words that describe nothing specific ("art", "design")
✏️ Writing Titles & Descriptions That Convert
The Perfect Title Formula
Formula: [Main Subject] + [Style/Color] + [Context] + [Format]

Examples:
  • ❌ Bad: "Beautiful design 2024"
  • ✅ Good: "Minimalist Blue Business Card Template with Geometric Pattern"
  • ❌ Bad: "Vector illustration set"
  • ✅ Good: "Flat Cartoon Coffee Shop Icons Set for Mobile App UI Design"
  • ❌ Bad: "Nice background"
  • ✅ Good: "Abstract Watercolor Blue Ocean Wave Background for Social Media Banner"
Title Rules
  • 50–200 characters is the ideal length
  • Start with the most important word (what buyers search for first)
  • Capitalize main nouns and adjectives
  • Never use ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation, or emojis
  • Never repeat the same word twice in the title
  • Include the style (flat, minimal, watercolor, 3D) — buyers search by style
Category Selection
Always choose the most specific category available. Wrong categories kill visibility. If unsure between two categories, pick the more niche one — less competition, more targeted buyers.
🚀 Building a Profitable Stock Portfolio
The Volume + Niche Formula
Two things must work together:
  • Volume: Upload at least 3–5 files per week consistently. The algorithm rewards active contributors with more visibility.
  • Niche focus: Pick 2–3 specific niches and dominate them. 200 files in one niche earns far more than 200 random files.
The 30-Day Rule
Adobe Stock takes time to index and rank new files. Upload seasonal content at least 30–45 days before the event:
  • Christmas designs → Upload by November 10
  • Eid designs → Upload 6 weeks before Eid
  • Valentine's Day → Upload by January 5
  • Use the Event Calendar tool to plan ahead automatically
Best Formats for Getting Accepted
  • Vectors (AI/EPS): Highest acceptance rate, most versatile, best long-term sales
  • JPEG: Great for lifestyle photos and raster artwork
  • PNG: Transparent backgrounds sell well as design elements
  • Video (MP4/MOV): Much less competition, significantly higher price point per download
Track, Analyze, Repeat
Check your Adobe Stock dashboard weekly. Find your top 10 files by views-to-download ratio. Make more files very similar to those. Remove or re-upload files with zero downloads after 90 days — improve the keywords and title first.
