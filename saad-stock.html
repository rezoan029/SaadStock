🎓 Stock Mentor
❌ Why Designs Get Rejected & How to Fix Them
Technical Quality Issues
Adobe Stock requires minimum 4MP resolution, sRGB color space, no compression artifacts, and clean vector paths with no open paths. JPEGs must be saved at maximum quality (10–12 in Photoshop).
  • Minimum 4 megapixels for raster files
  • Always embed sRGB color profile
  • Vectors: no raster effects, no open paths, all fonts outlined
  • No watermarks, logos, or signatures anywhere in the file
Poor or Misleading Keywords
Keyword rejection is one of the most common reasons. Use 10–50 relevant keywords. Adobe's AI detects irrelevant or misleading keywords instantly — this results in rejection or shadow-banning.
  • Start with the most specific, descriptive keywords first
  • Include subject, style, color, mood, use case, and industry
  • Never keyword stuff — it triggers automatic rejection
  • Include both singular and plural forms of key terms
Intellectual Property Issues
Recognizable brands, logos, trademarks, identifiable living people (without model release), and copyrighted artwork will always be rejected. Always get model releases for identifiable faces and property releases for private locations.
Over-Saturated Content
Adobe Stock has millions of generic sunsets, coffee cups, and business handshakes. These get accepted but never downloaded. Use the Market Gaps tool to find underserved niches before creating content.
🏷️ Advanced Keywording Strategy
The Keyword Pyramid Method
Structure keywords in 3 layers:
  • Layer 1 — Core (1–3 words): The exact subject. "businesswoman", "coffee cup", "geometric pattern"
  • Layer 2 — Context (5–10 words): Style, color, mood, setting. "minimal", "flat design", "blue background", "isolated on white"
  • Layer 3 — Use Case (10–20 words): Where buyers use it. "website banner", "social media post", "marketing material", "presentation slide"
High-Converting Keyword Categories
  • Season/Holiday: Christmas, summer, Eid, Diwali, Halloween, Ramadan, Easter
  • Design Style: minimalist, retro, watercolor, 3D render, flat icon, isometric
  • File Format: banner, background, icon set, infographic, template, pattern
  • Industry: healthcare, technology, education, finance, food, real estate
  • Emotion/Mood: happy, professional, peaceful, energetic, success, teamwork
Keywords to Always Avoid
Never use: brand names, celebrity names, overly generic words like "art" or "design" alone, misspellings, or keywords completely unrelated to your image. These either cause rejection or waste your keyword slots.
✏️ Writing Titles That Convert
The Perfect Title Formula
Formula: [Main Subject] + [Style/Color] + [Context/Use] + [Format]

Examples:
  • ❌ "Beautiful design 2024"
  • ✅ "Minimalist Blue Business Card Template with Geometric Pattern"
  • ❌ "Vector illustration"
  • ✅ "Flat Cartoon Coffee Shop Icons Set for Mobile App UI"
Title Rules
  • 50–200 characters is the ideal length
  • Start with the most important word (what buyers actually search for)
  • Include style keywords: flat, minimal, watercolor, 3D, cartoon, realistic
  • Never use ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation, or emoji in titles
  • Never repeat the same word twice in one title
  • Capitalize main nouns and adjectives (title case)
Category Selection
Always pick the most specific category available. Wrong categories hurt your visibility. The most niche category often has less competition and more targeted buyers — that's where you want to be.
🚀 Adobe Stock Growth Strategy
Volume + Niche = Success
Two things must work together:
  • Volume: Upload 3–5 new files per week minimum. Adobe's algorithm rewards active contributors with better visibility.
  • Niche focus: Pick 2–3 specific niches and dominate them. 200 files in one niche beats 200 random files every time.
The 30-Day Upload Rule
Adobe Stock's search takes time to index and rank new files. Upload seasonal content 30–45 days before the event. Christmas designs should be uploaded by November 10 to rank by December. Start early or lose the opportunity.
Best Formats by Opportunity
  • Vectors (AI/EPS): Highest acceptance rate, most versatile for buyers, preferred by designers
  • JPEG: Best for photos and raster artwork with no transparency needed
  • PNG: Great for transparent backgrounds, stickers, UI elements
  • Video (MP4/MOV): Less competition, significantly higher price per download
Track & Improve Weekly
Check your Adobe Stock dashboard every week. Find which files get the best view-to-download ratio. Make more content similar to your top performers. Re-upload files with zero downloads after 90 days with improved titles and keywords.
